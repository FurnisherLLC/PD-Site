# Email Outreach Template — Canopy Dynamics (revised)

## Context

Canopy Dynamics is a **drone prototyping firm**, not a manufacturer. We design, prototype, and set up manufacturing for other companies' heavy-lift drone programmes. We have in-house CFD capability but it's becoming a bottleneck as we take on more clients.

**The pitch narrative:**
- We prototype heavy-lift drones for other companies (agriculture & defence)
- We do the full scope: design → prototype → manufacturing setup
- All-Canadian engineering and supply chain
- We have our own in-house CFD (rotor wake analysis, drag profiling, etc.)
- BUT: we're getting more business than we can handle
- The CFD work is bottlenecking our mechanical engineers
- It's become a cost centre and we're looking to outsource it
- We want sample packages / benchmarks for small quadcopters with heavy underslung payloads

**Key rule:** The website should show CFD as a capability we provide (one line in the capabilities list). The email is where we reveal it's becoming a problem.

## Template (cold email)

Hi [Name/Team],

I'm reaching out from Canopy Dynamics — we're a Canadian engineering firm that prototypes heavy-lift drones for other companies in agriculture and defence. We do the full scope: airframe design, composites, avionics integration, flight testing, and manufacturing setup. We've delivered about 25 prototypes across three platform families, with 15+ active client engagements.

We maintain an in-house CFD capability for rotorcraft external aerodynamics — rotor wake interaction, downloadwash, drag profiling, thermal management. The work has been solid, but frankly it's becoming a bottleneck for us.

Our mechanical engineers are spending more and more time running CFD cycles instead of doing the design and integration work they're best at. We've been taking on more clients this year than we expected, and what started as a useful in-house tool has turned into a cost centre that's pulling our team off the critical path.

We're looking to outsource our CFD work to a specialist firm that can handle regular rotorcraft analysis cycles. Specifically, the kind of work we'd be sending your way:

- **Rotor-on-rotor wake interaction** for 4-rotor heavy-lift quadcopter configurations (MTOW 12–25 kg)
- **Downloadwash analysis** to inform precision-ag spraying altitude (3–5 m AGL target, underslung payload)
- **Drag profiling** for configurations with underslung payloads (4–12 kg, various form factors)
- **Thermal management** simulation for enclosed avionics bays in high-ambient operating conditions

We'd love to see a sample package or benchmark case you can share — something for a small quadcopter with a heavy underslung load. We can run the same geometry through our in-house solver on the other end and compare results. If you have standard test cases or a "here's what we did for a similar platform" example, that would be very helpful.

Happy to sign an NDA and share our current reference airframe geometry (STEP format) if you'd prefer a live comparison over benchmark data.

Do you have sample packages or benchmark cases you can share?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
Head of Engineering
Canopy Dynamics
engineering@canopydynamics.ca
canopydynamics.ca

## Response handling

**If they ask for airframe geometry / STEP files:**
> Happy to share our CD-12 Ag reference prototype geometry under NDA. It's a quadcopter — ~1200mm motor-to-motor, 25 kg MTOW, underslung spray tank. Let me know your preferred format and I'll send the NDA our end.

**If they quote a price:**
> That's within our expected range for an initial validation phase. If the benchmarks are competitive with our in-house results, we'd be looking at retainer-based recurring work — likely 2–3 analysis cycles per month across our active programmes.

**If they ask about your current clients:**
> We can share sector-level detail (agriculture technology companies, defence prime subcontractors, precision-ag cooperatives) but client names are under NDA. Happy to share aggregate case studies showing the kind of airframes we've developed.

**If they ask about your in-house solver:**
> We've built and tuned an OpenFOAM-based workflow with custom rotor boundary conditions. It works well — the issue is that maintaining and running it is eating our engineers' time on active programmes. That's why we're looking to offload it.

**If they ask to visit:**
> We're in Kitchener, Ontario. Available most weekdays — shoot me some dates and I'll set aside time to walk you through our current prototypes and the kind of CFD work we'd be sending over.
